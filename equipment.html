Equipment

The Group employs a range of specialised equipment selected for reliability, modularity, and effectiveness across varied operational environments.

Small Arms

Operators are issued the C8 Carbine (L119A2) as the primary weapon system, selected for its accuracy, compact dimensions, and compatibility with suppressors. Secondary weapons include the Glock 17 pistol. Support weapons include the L7A2 General Purpose Machine Gun and the M72 Light Anti-Armour Weapon for anti-structural operations.

Optics & Sensors

Weapon systems are typically fitted with SpecterOS 4x optical sights or EOTech holographic sights depending on mission requirements. Night observation devices include the PVS-14 monocular and panoramic night vision goggles. Thermal imaging capabilities are provided at team level for target acquisition in zero-visibility conditions.

Mobility

Ground mobility relies on the Supacat HMT 400 (Jackal) for long-range insertion and the Polaris MRZR for discrete reconnaissance. Aviation assets include the Chinook HC6 for heavy lift and tactical insertion, and the Merlin HC3 for medium lift operations. Maritime capabilities include rigid inflatable boats and kayaks for clandestine waterborne insertion.

Support Equipment

Communications are provided by the PRC-343 personal role radio for intra-team comms and the PRC-117 for long-range transmission. Breaching capabilities include explosive charges for rapid entry and hydraulic tools for controlled access. Every operator carries an individual first aid kit (IFAK) with haemostatic agents and tourniquets, plus team-level casualty evacuation equipment.

Procurement Philosophy

Equipment is selected based on operational requirement rather than cost or availability. We maintain equipment at higher states of readiness than conventional forces, with weapons zeroed and equipment packed for immediate deployment. Every operator maintains personal kit to the highest standard; equipment failure in training is treated as seriously as failure in operations.
