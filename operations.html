Operations

The Task Force Orion conducts four primary lines of operation, each requiring distinct capabilities and training pathways.

Direct Action

Short-duration strikes and other small-scale offensive actions conducted as a special operation in hostile, denied, or politically sensitive environments. These operations differ from conventional offensive actions in the level of physical and political risk, the degree of discrimination in the use of force, and the requirement for precise application of force.

  • High-value target acquisition
  • Infrastructure interdiction
  • Hostage rescue operations
  • Precision raids on fortified positions

Special Reconnaissance

Reconnaissance and surveillance actions conducted at depth to collect or verify information of strategic or operational significance. These operations are characterised by clandestine insertion, extended duration, and the requirement to avoid detection rather than engage in combat.

  • Covert observation posts
  • Environmental preparation
  • Battle damage assessment
  • Strategic intelligence collection

Support & Enablement

Training, advising, and operating alongside partner forces to build indigenous capacity. This includes the selection, training, and operational mentoring of foreign military units, as well as the provision of force protection and tactical leadership during combined operations.

  • Partner force training
  • Tactical advice and liaison
  • Combined operations leadership
  • Capability development

Aviation Operations

The integration of rotary-wing assets to provide tactical mobility, precision insertion, and logistical support to special operations. This includes air assault operations, fast-rope deployment, and the coordination of close air support in austere environments.

  • Air assault raids
  • Tactical air mobility
  • Rotary wing insertion/extraction
  • Casualty evacuation

Operational Tempo

The Group maintains a continuous cycle of readiness, training, and deployment. While specific operations are classified, the unit typically conducts no-notice deployment exercises weekly and maintains a four-hour recall capability for counter-terrorism response. Overseas training deployments occur monthly to maintain currency in varied terrain and climate conditions.
