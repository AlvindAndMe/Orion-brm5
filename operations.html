Operational Doctrine & Tasking

This page outlines the principal operations the unit conducts, the tasking model, and how elements are employed in theatre.

Tasking model

Operations are planned using a modular tasking model: small teams for reconnaissance, larger elements for direct action, and integrated support for sustainment. Each task follows a strict planning cycle: Intelligence → Plan → Rehearse → Execute → Assess.

Core operation types

Direct Action

Short, decisive strikes against high-value targets.

Precision planning, close-quarters tactics, and rapid extraction are standard.

Special Reconnaissance

Covert observation and reporting to support operational decisions.

Long-range patrols, overwatch, and HUMINT collection are emphasized.

Counter‑terror & Hostage Rescue

High-risk rescue and containment operations.

Requires joint planning with medical and negotiation assets where available.

Integration with support

Operations are never standalone. Aviation, logistics, medevac, and intelligence elements are integrated at planning stage to ensure mission success and force protection.

After-action & lessons learned

Every operation concludes with a structured AAR (After Action Review). Findings feed training cycles and doctrine updates to maintain readiness.
