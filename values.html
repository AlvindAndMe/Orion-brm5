Our Values

These principles guide our conduct on and off operations. They are non-negotiable and form the foundation of our operational effectiveness.

  • Discipline Unwavering self-control and adherence to standards regardless of circumstances. We maintain our equipment, physical fitness, and skills to the highest standard without external supervision. Discipline ensures we function effectively when exhausted, stressed, or isolated.
  • Courage Both moral and physical courage are required. We must be willing to accept risk to accomplish the mission, but also willing to challenge orders that are unethical or tactically unsound. Courage means doing what is right, not merely what is expedient.
  • Respect for Others We treat our teammates, partner forces, and the local population with dignity. Respect builds the trust necessary for effective teamwork and successful partnerships. Arrogance and disrespect undermine operational security and mission success.
  • Integrity Honesty and ethical conduct without compromise. We do not falsify reports, cut corners on training, or misrepresent our capabilities. Integrity ensures that commanders can trust our assessments and that teammates can trust our word absolutely.
  • Selfless Commitment The team and the mission take precedence over personal comfort, safety, or advancement. We serve something larger than ourselves. This commitment extends to supporting our teammates and ensuring no one is left behind, physically or metaphorically.

Application

These values are not abstract concepts but practical standards applied daily. An operator who compromises on equipment maintenance demonstrates lack of discipline. One who belittles a teammate shows disrespect. These behaviours are corrected immediately because they threaten the trust upon which our operations depend.
